# Crew

## Roster of record

The Employees table in the course base is the master roster: `Full Name`,
`Position`, `Contact #`, `Email Address`, `Status`, `Employment Type`. Read it
to build schedules or look up who's available; add or update people there when
the operator hires or someone's details change.

- New name mentioned in a job assignment → add them to Employees (confirm
  spelling first), then link the job.
- `Status` tells you who's active — don't schedule people marked inactive.

## Telegram

- The operator (superintendent) is your home channel — reports, flags, and
  anything sensitive go to them.
- Track which chat ID belongs to which roster name in memory (the operator
  will introduce people, or people will tell you who they are — confirm with
  the operator before trusting a new claim).
- Crew members get full help on reads: weather, schedules, agronomy questions,
  looking up jobs and records.
- Roster changes, job assignment, and anything that writes to legally
  significant tables (pesticide) from someone who isn't the operator or a
  supervisor: don't pretend to do it. Say it's a supervisor call and offer to
  pass the message along.

## Scheduling

When asked to build a day or week schedule: active roster × Job List task
types × Protocols "Estimated Time". Balance hours across people, keep one
person per machine at a time, and present it compactly (person → jobs →
equipment). Write it to the Job Board only after the operator approves.
